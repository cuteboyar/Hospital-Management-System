\section{Introduction}

This document presents the formal Z notation model for the Hospital Management System. The model defines system states, invariants, and operations for managing patients, doctors, and appointments.

\section{Basic Sets}

\[
[PATIENT, DOCTOR]
\]

\section{State Schema}

\[
\begin{schema}{HospitalSystem}
patients : \mathcal{P} \ PATIENT \\
doctors : \mathcal{P} \ DOCTOR \\
appointments : PATIENT \leftrightarrow DOCTOR
\where
dom \ appointments \subseteq patients \\
ran \ appointments \subseteq doctors
\end{schema}
\]

\section{Initial State}

\[
\begin{schema}{InitHospitalSystem}
HospitalSystem
\where
patients = \emptyset \\
doctors = \emptyset \\
appointments = \emptyset
\end{schema}
\]

\section{Operations}

\subsection{RegisterPatient}

\[
\begin{schema}{RegisterPatient}
\Delta HospitalSystem \\
newPatient? : PATIENT
\where
newPatient? \notin patients \\
patients' = patients \cup \{newPatient?\} \\
doctors' = doctors \\
appointments' = appointments
\end{schema}
\]

\subsection{AddDoctor}

\[
\begin{schema}{AddDoctor}
\Delta HospitalSystem \\
newDoctor? : DOCTOR
\where
newDoctor? \notin doctors \\
doctors' = doctors \cup \{newDoctor?\} \\
patients' = patients \\
appointments' = appointments
\end{schema}
\]

\subsection{BookAppointment}

\[
\begin{schema}{BookAppointment}
\Delta HospitalSystem \\
p? : PATIENT \\
d? : DOCTOR
\where
p? \in patients \\
d? \in doctors \\
appointments' = appointments \cup \{(p?, d?)\} \\
patients' = patients \\
doctors' = doctors
\end{schema}
\]

\subsection{CancelAppointment}

\[
\begin{schema}{CancelAppointment}
\Delta HospitalSystem \\
p? : PATIENT \\
d? : DOCTOR
\where
(p?, d?) \in appointments \\
appointments' = appointments \setminus \{(p?, d?)\} \\
patients' = patients \\
doctors' = doctors
\end{schema}
\]

\section{State Transition Example}

Before appointment booking:

\[
appointments = \emptyset
\]

After appointment booking:

\[
appointments = \{(Ali, DrAhsan)\}
\]

The Z notation model formally specifies the Hospital Management System using states, invariants, and operations. The model ensures consistency and correctness of patient, doctor, and appointment management.
